CONSTRUCTION AGREEMENT

This CONSTRUCTION AGREEMENT (the "Agreement") is made and entered into on January 15, 2025 (the "Effective Date"), by and between:

ABC REAL ESTATE LLC, a corporation organized and existing under the laws of the State of California, with its principal place of business at 123 Main Street, Los Angeles, CA 90001 (hereinafter referred to as the "Owner"),

and

XYZ BUILDERS INC., a corporation organized and existing under the laws of the State of California, with its principal place of business at 456 Construction Avenue, Los Angeles, CA 90002 (hereinafter referred to as the "Contractor").

The Owner and Contractor may be individually referred to as a "Party" and collectively as the "Parties."

WHEREAS, the Owner desires to construct a commercial retail facility known as "Sunshine Mall" at 789 Retail Road, Los Angeles, CA 90003 (the "Project");

WHEREAS, the Contractor has the necessary expertise, licenses, and resources to construct the Project in accordance with the Contract Documents;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements herein contained, the Parties agree as follows:

ARTICLE 1: CONTRACT DOCUMENTS

1.1 The Contract Documents consist of this Agreement, the Project Plans and Specifications dated December 1, 2024 (the "Plans"), Project Schedule dated December 15, 2024 (the "Schedule"), General Conditions, Special Conditions, and all Addenda issued prior to and all Modifications issued after execution of this Agreement. These documents form the Contract and are incorporated by reference as if fully set forth herein.

1.2 In the event of any inconsistency among the Contract Documents, the order of precedence shall be: (1) Modifications, (2) this Agreement, (3) Special Conditions, (4) General Conditions, (5) Plans and Specifications, and (6) other documents included in the Contract Documents.

ARTICLE 2: SCOPE OF WORK

2.1 The Contractor shall furnish all labor, materials, equipment, tools, supervision, and other facilities required to complete the Project as described in the Contract Documents.

2.2 The Contractor shall complete all work in strict accordance with the Contract Documents and all applicable laws, ordinances, rules, regulations, and orders of any public authority having jurisdiction over the Project.

ARTICLE 3: CONTRACT SUM

3.1 The Owner shall pay the Contractor for the performance of the Contract, subject to additions and deductions provided therein, the Contract Sum of Twenty-Five Million Dollars ($25,000,000.00).

3.2 The Contract Sum is based on the following alternates, which are described in the Contract Documents and are hereby accepted by the Owner: None.

ARTICLE 4: TIME OF COMMENCEMENT AND SUBSTANTIAL COMPLETION

4.1 The Work shall commence on February 1, 2025 (the "Commencement Date") or on such other date as may be established in a Notice to Proceed issued by the Owner.

4.2 The Contractor shall achieve Substantial Completion of the entire Work not later than December 31, 2026 (the "Substantial Completion Date"), subject to adjustments of the Contract Time as provided in the Contract Documents.

4.3 The following milestones are established as part of the Project Schedule:
   a. Milestone 1: Site Preparation and Foundation Work - May 31, 2025
   b. Milestone 2: Steel Framework Complete - August 15, 2025
   c. Milestone 3: Building Envelope Complete - January 31, 2026
   d. Milestone 4: Interior Build-Out Complete - September 30, 2026
   e. Milestone 5: Substantial Completion - December 31, 2026

ARTICLE 5: PROGRESS PAYMENTS

5.1 Based upon Applications for Payment submitted to the Owner's Representative by the Contractor, the Owner shall make progress payments on account of the Contract Sum to the Contractor as provided below and elsewhere in the Contract Documents.

5.2 The period covered by each Application for Payment shall be one calendar month ending on the last day of the month.

5.3 The Owner shall make payment to the Contractor not later than thirty (30) days after the Owner's Representative receives the Application for Payment.

5.4 Each Application for Payment shall be based on the schedule of values submitted by the Contractor in accordance with the Contract Documents.

5.5 The Owner shall retain five percent (5%) of each progress payment until Substantial Completion of the Work.

ARTICLE 6: FORCE MAJEURE

6.1 Definition. "Force Majeure" means an event that is beyond the reasonable control of a Party, including but not limited to acts of God, earthquake, fire, explosion, storm, flood, labor disputes, war, terrorist acts, epidemics, pandemics, government orders, embargoes, blockades, and regional material shortages.

6.2 Relief. If a Party is prevented from performing any of its obligations due to Force Majeure, such Party shall be excused from performance or timely performance, as the case may be, of such obligations for so long as such circumstances prevail and such Party continues to use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact of the Force Majeure event and resume performance as soon as possible.

6.3 Notice. The Party affected by Force Majeure shall notify the other Party as soon as reasonably practicable of the occurrence of Force Majeure, its impact on performance, and its anticipated duration. The affected Party shall update the other Party regarding the situation on a weekly basis.

6.4 Extension of Time. If the Contractor is delayed at any time in the commencement or progress of the Work by Force Majeure events, then the Contract Time shall be extended for such reasonable time as the Owner's Representative determines.

6.5 Costs. In the case of a Force Majeure event that results in an extension of the Contract Time, the Contractor shall be entitled to reasonable additional costs incurred during the extension period directly attributable to the Force Majeure event, subject to proper documentation and the Owner's approval, which shall not be unreasonably withheld.

ARTICLE 7: LIQUIDATED DAMAGES

7.1 The Owner and Contractor recognize that time is of the essence in this Agreement and that the Owner will suffer significant financial loss if the Work is not substantially completed by the Substantial Completion Date set forth above.

7.2 Accordingly, the Parties agree that if the Contractor fails to achieve Substantial Completion of the Work by the Substantial Completion Date, as adjusted pursuant to the Contract Documents, the Contractor shall pay the Owner, as liquidated damages and not as a penalty, the sum of Ten Thousand Dollars ($10,000.00) per calendar day until Substantial Completion is achieved.

7.3 In addition, the Contractor shall pay the Owner, as liquidated damages and not as a penalty, the sum of Five Thousand Dollars ($5,000.00) per calendar day for each milestone not achieved by the date specified in Section 4.3, as adjusted pursuant to the Contract Documents.

7.4 The Parties agree that the amounts set forth above are a reasonable estimate of the damages the Owner would incur in the event of delay and would be difficult or impossible to determine with certainty.

ARTICLE 8: CHANGES IN THE WORK

8.1 The Owner, without invalidating the Contract, may order changes in the Work consisting of additions, deletions, or modifications, with the Contract Sum and Contract Time being adjusted accordingly.

8.2 All such changes in the Work shall be authorized by a written Change Order signed by the Owner and Contractor.

8.3 The Contract Sum and Contract Time may be changed only by Change Order.

8.4 The cost or credit to the Owner from a change in the Work shall be determined by mutual agreement between the Owner and Contractor.

ARTICLE 9: INSURANCE AND BONDS

9.1 The Contractor shall purchase and maintain insurance and provide bonds as set forth in the Contract Documents.

9.2 The Contractor shall deliver the required bonds to the Owner not later than the date of execution of this Agreement.

ARTICLE 10: DISPUTE RESOLUTION

10.1 Mediation. Any claim, dispute, or other matter in question arising out of or related to the Contract shall be subject to mediation as a condition precedent to binding dispute resolution. The Parties shall endeavor to resolve their claims by mediation, which shall be conducted in accordance with the Construction Industry Mediation Rules of the American Arbitration Association.

10.2 Arbitration. If the Parties do not resolve their dispute through mediation, the dispute shall be settled by binding arbitration administered by the American Arbitration Association in accordance with its Construction Industry Arbitration Rules.

10.3 Venue. Unless otherwise agreed in writing, all mediation and arbitration proceedings shall take place in Los Angeles, California.

ARTICLE 11: TERMINATION OR SUSPENSION

11.1 The Contract may be terminated by the Owner or the Contractor as provided in the General Conditions.

11.2 The Work may be suspended by the Owner as provided in the General Conditions.

ARTICLE 12: MISCELLANEOUS PROVISIONS

12.1 The Contract shall be governed by the law of the State of California.

12.2 The Contractor shall not assign the Contract without written consent of the Owner.

12.3 Notice. All notices required under this Agreement shall be in writing and shall be deemed properly served if delivered in person to the individual or member of the firm or entity or to an officer of the corporation for whom they are intended, or if delivered at or sent by registered or certified mail to the last business address known to the party giving notice.

12.4 Waiver. The waiver by either Party of any breach of any term, covenant, or condition contained in this Agreement shall not be deemed to be a waiver of any subsequent breach of the same or any other term, covenant, or condition contained herein.
